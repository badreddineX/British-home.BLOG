The idea that a meaningful room makeover requires thousands of pounds is one of the most persistent myths in home design. Some of the most impactful transformations I've seen — and done — were achieved for under £200 and a weekend of focused work.

Here is an exact plan: what to buy, what to do, and in what order.

---

## The Budget

| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Paint (2.5L, one feature wall or full room in small space) | £25–£40 |
| Cushion covers (4×, mixing two sizes) | £30–£50 |
| Throw | £20–£35 |
| Rug (if needed — skip if you have a good one) | £40–£80 |
| Plants (2–3, mix of sizes) | £15–£25 |
| Candles or diffuser | £10–£15 |
| Rearranging books / accessories you already own | £0 |
| **Total** | **£140–£245** |

This budget is deliberately flexible — you almost certainly have some of these things already, which frees budget for others.

---

## Friday Evening: Plan and Prepare

Before spending any money, do this:

**Audit what you have.** Go through every cushion, throw, candle, book, picture frame, and ornament in the house. Remove everything from the living room. This sounds extreme but it's the most important step — you can only see what works when you see what exists.

**Identify the problem.** Why does the room feel wrong? Options:
- It feels cluttered
- It feels cold and unlived-in
- It looks dated
- It lacks a focal point
- The furniture arrangement isn't working

The answer informs every purchase.

**Take photos.** Before anything. You'll want the comparison.

---

## Saturday Morning: The Big Clean and Rearrange

Start with a thorough clean — skirting boards, window sills, behind the sofa, inside the fireplace if you have one. A clean room looks better before anything else changes.

Then rearrange the furniture. The most common improvements:
- Pull the sofa away from the wall (even 30cm makes a difference)
- Angle two armchairs slightly toward each other and toward the sofa
- Clear the floor completely — nothing on it except the rug and furniture

Spend an hour trying different arrangements before committing. This costs nothing and is often the most impactful change.

---

## Saturday Afternoon: Paint

If you're painting, do it now. One well-chosen feature wall — behind the sofa, the fireplace wall, or the TV wall — can completely reframe a room.

**Top choices for this year:**
- *Sage green* (Dulux Timeless Tranquility or Crown Soft Sage): universally flattering
- *Warm terracotta* (Crown Warm Clay or Wilko Terracotta Blush): bold but surprisingly liveable
- *Deep navy* (Dulux Midnight Blue or Farrow & Ball Hague Blue): dramatic and rich
- *Warm charcoal* (Crown Smoked Glass): sophisticated, reads expensive

One 2.5L tester can do a small feature wall. Buy a proper brush and cut the edges carefully — rushed painting looks rushed.

---

## Saturday Evening: Soft Furnishings

While paint dries:

**Cushions.** The rule is odd numbers look better (3 or 5 on a sofa), and mixing one pattern with two or three plains makes a more interesting arrangement than all plains or all patterns. Choose a colour from the paint, or choose cushions first and paint to match.

**The throw.** Drape it casually over one arm of the sofa or fold it across a corner. Chunky knit and soft waffle weave both photograph and live well. Dunelm has excellent options from £20.

---

## Sunday Morning: Accessories and Plants

**Edit brutally.** Return only what earns its place on the shelves and surfaces. Group things in threes: tall + medium + small. Use books as risers — a stack of hardbacks gives a plant or candle a bit of height.

**Plants transform a room.** A tall plant (a fiddle-leaf fig or bird of paradise), a medium trailing plant (pothos or heartleaf philodendron), and a small plant on a surface (a succulent or mini cactus) at three different heights creates a sense of life and abundance. IKEA and Patch Plants are good sources.

---

## Sunday Afternoon: The Finishing Details

- Light all the candles and take stock of the room from the doorway
- Adjust the rug so front furniture legs sit on it
- Check lighting — add a lamp if the room feels flat in the evening
- Take the after photos

---

## The Honest Truth

A £200 makeover doesn't add an extension or replace the kitchen. It works with what's already there and makes it feel intentional. The biggest gains come from editing (removing), arranging (free), and adding one or two well-chosen things that pull the room together.

Most living rooms have too much in them, arranged in a way that just sort of happened over time. Resetting that, deliberately, is almost always an improvement.
