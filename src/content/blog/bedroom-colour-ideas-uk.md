Choosing a bedroom colour is one of those decisions that feels small but genuinely affects how you feel every day. The bedroom is the first thing you see in the morning and the last thing you see at night. It deserves proper consideration.

In British homes, the key constraint is light. Our natural light is cool and often diffused — which means some colours that look beautiful in sunnier climates look flat and sad here.

---

## Warm Neutrals: The Safe and Successful Choice

Warm neutrals are the most consistently successful choice in UK bedrooms. They work in almost any orientation and pair with almost any soft furnishing.

**Best picks:**
- Farrow & Ball *Elephant's Breath* — the iconic British warm grey-taupe
- Dulux *Nutmeg White* — a softer, cream-edged option
- Little Greene *Slaked Lime Mid* — beautiful chalky warmth
- Crown *Warm Cream* — excellent budget option around £18 per 2.5L

Pair with bedding in the same tonal family (warm linen, oatmeal cotton) and accents in deeper earthy tones.

---

## Soft Greens: The Bedroom Trend That's Here to Stay

Soft, muted sage and eucalyptus greens have dominated interior design for several years and continue to feel fresh and calming. They work particularly well in bedrooms because they're grounding without being heavy.

**Best picks:**
- Farrow & Ball *Mizzle* — a complex sage with grey and green in equal measure
- Farrow & Ball *Pale Powder* — lighter, more blue-green
- Dulux *Timeless Tranquility* — a beautiful affordable equivalent
- Annie Sloan Chalk Paint *Olive* — for painted furniture in a different tone

---

## Deep, Dramatic Darks

Dark bedrooms have a quality that pale rooms simply can't replicate — they feel like proper sanctuary. Used correctly, a dark bedroom feels rich and restful rather than oppressive.

**Best picks for dark bedrooms:**
- Farrow & Ball *Hague Blue* — rich navy with green undertones
- Farrow & Ball *Down Pipe* — the iconic dark grey-charcoal
- Dulux *Midnight Teal* — deep, luxurious, very affordable
- B&Q *Dark Forest* — excellent value option

**Tips for making dark work:**
- Paint ceiling and walls the same colour — don't leave the ceiling white
- Increase the warmth of your lighting significantly
- Choose bedding and curtains in lighter tones for contrast

---

## Soft Blues and Blue-Greys

Blue is traditionally associated with calm and sleep — and in the right tone, it really does work. Avoid anything too cool or too bright.

**Best picks:**
- Farrow & Ball *Borrowed Light* — one of their most popular ever, for good reason
- Dulux *Morning Mist* — soft and widely available
- Little Greene *Pale Lupin* — a gentle blue with warmth in it

---

## What to Avoid

- **Pure white or brilliant white** — clinical and cold
- **Warm red or orange** — too stimulating for sleep
- **Anything with a purple undertone** — looks artificial in British light
- **Very cool greys** — feel bleak rather than calm in lower-light rooms

---

## The One-Third Rule

Whatever colour you choose for the walls, keep the rest of the room to one-third of that intensity or less. Deep navy walls? Pale linen bedding and light wood furniture. Sage green walls? White woodwork and cream curtains. The contrast is what makes the wall colour feel intentional rather than overwhelming.

---

## Tester Pots: The Non-Negotiable Step

Every paint brand sells tester pots. Buy at least two you're considering. Paint 30cm × 30cm patches on different walls and observe them at morning, midday, and evening before deciding. This £3–£6 investment prevents a £200 mistake.
