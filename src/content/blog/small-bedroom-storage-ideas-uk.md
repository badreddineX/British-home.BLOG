Small bedrooms are one of the defining realities of British housing. Victorian terraces, converted flats, and new-build houses all tend to have at least one bedroom where fitting a wardrobe feels like solving a puzzle.

The good news: most small bedrooms have significant untapped storage potential. These solutions don't require building work — just some thought and some well-chosen products.

---

## Start with the Bed

The bed occupies more floor space than anything else in the room. Make it earn that space.

**Ottoman beds** are the single best storage investment for a small bedroom. They lift on gas pistons to reveal a substantial, easily accessible storage area underneath. John Lewis, Dreams, and IKEA all sell good ottoman beds across a range of budgets (£300–£1,200).

If you already have a bed you love, **bed risers** (around £15–£20 from Amazon) combined with flat storage boxes create the same effect for a fraction of the cost.

---

## Go Vertical with the Wardrobe

The space above a standard wardrobe is almost always wasted. If you're buying new, go for a wardrobe that reaches the ceiling — or add a secondary box unit on top of an existing wardrobe.

Within the wardrobe:
- Add a second hanging rail below clothes that don't need full-length hanging space
- Use shelf dividers to prevent jumper piles from collapsing
- Vacuum storage bags compress bulky seasonal items to a fraction of their size

---

## The Alcove Opportunity

If your bedroom has an alcove (common in Victorian and Edwardian houses), it's prime storage real estate. Options:
- Fitted shelving with a curtain rail across the front — looks built-in, costs very little
- A tall narrow IKEA PAX wardrobe fitted into the alcove
- Deep shelves at ceiling height with a ladder for access

Alcoves beside a chimney breast are often overlooked as potential wardrobes. They're perfectly proportioned for hanging clothes if you add a rail and a curtain.

---

## Hooks Are Underrated

A row of hooks on the back of the bedroom door holds:
- Tomorrow's outfit
- Bags and accessories
- Belts and scarves
- A dressing gown

This removes at least half a drawer's worth of frequently accessed items from storage. Brass hooks from Victorian Plumbing or simple IKEA TJUSIG hooks work beautifully and take ten minutes to install.

---

## Bedside Table Alternatives

Standard bedside tables have one small drawer and a surface. Consider alternatives that offer more:
- A small chest of drawers beside the bed (more storage, same footprint)
- A floating shelf — no floor space at all
- A ladder shelf (small footprint, multiple levels)

---

## Drawer Dividers

The biggest enemy of small space storage isn't lack of space — it's disorganisation expanding to fill available space. Drawer dividers transform chaotic drawers into properly functional ones. IKEA SKUBB inserts cost around £5 and fit most standard drawers.

---

## The Capsule Wardrobe Approach

No amount of clever storage solves a wardrobe with three times more clothes than you wear. A seasonal edit — keeping only what you wore in the last 12 months — typically frees 30–40% of wardrobe space without any new furniture at all.

A small bedroom with good storage isn't about cramming more in — it's about finding a place for everything, so that everything stays where it belongs.
